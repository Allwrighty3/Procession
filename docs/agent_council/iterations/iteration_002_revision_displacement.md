## Standing role views

### Learner Advocate

- **Supported explanation:** the learner can accumulate support but lacks an effective local process for revising support after its consequences change.
- **Counterevidence:** Iteration 001 does not distinguish failure to weaken obsolete support from failure to strengthen and displace it with successful competing support.
- **Distinguishing experiment:** compare local contradiction-sensitive weakening against competition-only displacement while holding the world and observation stream fixed.

### Teacher and Environment Advocate

- **Supported explanation:** the learner may still lack sufficiently informative experience, but simply extending exposure did not help.
- **Counterevidence:** C0 and V1 corrected at the same rate despite doubled post-reversal opportunity; V2 did not improve with doubled acquisition exposure.
- **Distinguishing experiment:** preserve the exact association-reversal scenario and vary only how the learner updates internal support. No new teacher, cue, reward, or environmental guidance should be introduced.

### Emergence Guardian

- **Shortcut risks:** naming an action as obsolete, exposing the reversal, supplying a correct-action label, directly identifying the responsible pathway, or applying a semantic punishment would manufacture revision rather than permit it to emerge.
- **Protected information boundaries:** coordinates, correct directions, reversal state, observer classifications, causal explanations, and success labels remain unavailable to the entity.
- **Acceptable local information:** an active local trace, its retained expected continuation, the immediately sensed continuation, and ordinary support generated by successful local activity.

### Measurement Skeptic

- **Metric risks:** a lower obsolete-action rate can result from inactivity rather than useful revision; pathway weakening may erase useful learning generally; correction may occur only because one mechanism changes action frequency.
- **Confounds:** different random histories, different scenario timing, different actuator availability, observer labels entering learner updates, or unbounded parameter tuning.
- **Required controls and seed coverage:** paired seeds `1..100`; identical world schedule and entity-visible stream; report correction rate and delay, normalized obsolete-action rate, activity rate, intake, support removed, support deposited, and pathway re-use after a later restoration probe.

### OTP and Architecture Guardian

- **Elixir/OTP boundaries:** Elixir remains authoritative over scenario execution, learner state, validation, deterministic seeding, and metrics generation.
- **Blueprint/live-process implications:** none; this is an isolated simulation experiment and must not alter world spawning or persistence architecture.
- **Implementation boundary:** add experiment-local validated update modes around the existing association-reversal learner substrate. Do not replace the core learner or promote a mode into default behavior.

### Adjudicator

- **Agreements:** Iteration 001 weakens opportunity, acquisition exposure, attribution-only, and measurement-disagreement explanations. The next experiment should vary learner revision behavior, not create a new world.
- **Disagreements retained:** the council cannot yet determine whether direct contradiction weakening is necessary, whether competitive replacement is sufficient, or whether both will fail because revision requires a deeper substrate change.
- **Selected bounded question:** In the unchanged association-reversal scenario, does adaptation improve when locally contradicted support can weaken, when successful competing activity can displace obsolete support without direct punishment, or only when both processes are available?
- **Deferred questions and why:** passive time-based decay is deferred because it does not distinguish experience-driven revision from forgetting and could erase stable useful learning. Multi-reversal lifetime learning is deferred until one bounded revision process demonstrates measurable value.

## Experiment specification

- **Control — C0 current:** existing local-adaptive learner behavior with no new weakening or displacement rule.
- **Variant V1 — contradiction weakening:** when an active local trace has a retained expected continuation and the immediately sensed continuation differs, reduce only support attributable to that active trace. No correct alternative is supplied.
- **Variant V2 — competitive displacement:** do not directly weaken contradicted support. When a competing local trace produces ordinary successful support, allow finite support competition to displace support from mutually competing active alternatives within the same local choice context.
- **Variant V3 — combined:** apply both bounded contradiction weakening and competitive displacement.
- **Fixed conditions:** the same association-reversal world, pre/post schedule, sensory channels, actuator pressures, attribution mode, action availability, initial learner state, and observer logic for all variants.
- **Seeds/sample size:** paired deterministic seeds `1..100`, four variants, 400 histories. A deterministic replay must produce byte-identical raw rows.
- **Observer-only measurements:** behavioral correction and delay; resistance correction and delay; normalized obsolete-action rate; activity and inactivity; intake; support weakened by contradiction; support displaced by competition; competing support deposited; support distribution before and after reversal; metric agreement; restoration-probe reuse.
- **Entity-visible information:** ordinary local sensory continuation, active local trace state already available to the learner, and ordinary consequences of executed activity. No observer labels.
- **Restoration probe:** after the primary post-reversal window, restore the original contingency for a short fixed observer-controlled phase. This tests whether V1/V3 destroyed prior learning or merely reduced its active dominance. The entity receives no restoration flag.
- **Success criterion:** at least one treatment variant improves paired behavioral correction over C0 in at least `20/100` seeds, worsens in no more than `10/100`, lowers median normalized obsolete-action rate by at least `0.15`, and does not reduce median activity or intake by more than `10%`. Its restoration-probe recovery must remain within `20%` of C0 to avoid counting broad erasure as successful revision.
- **Failure criterion:** V1, V2, and V3 each improve paired behavioral correction in fewer than `10/100` seeds and lower median obsolete-action rate by less than `0.05`, or apparent improvement is explained by inactivity/intake collapse.
- **Mechanism distinction:** contradiction weakening is supported over competition when V1 materially outperforms V2; competition is supported over contradiction when V2 materially outperforms V1; interaction is supported when V3 materially exceeds both single-process variants under the success safeguards.
- **Inconclusive criterion:** correction improves but activity, intake, or restoration safeguards fail; single-process variants conflict across primary metrics; deterministic replay fails; or fewer than 100 valid paired seeds remain.
- **Falsifiers:** no measurable support changes occur in the treatment paths; learner-visible state includes observer-only labels; scenario histories differ across paired variants before update behavior is applied; correction is produced through inactivity; or restoration reveals indiscriminate erasure.
- **Authorized files/modules if Level 2 is granted:** one isolated experiment module, one Mix metrics task, focused ExUnit tests, one dedicated/manual GitHub Actions workflow or extension of the Iteration evidence workflow, and iteration-specific documentation/state updates.
- **Forbidden changes:** changing default learner behavior; modifying core world physics; adding semantic reward, reversal flags, correct-action labels, named obsolete actions, global causal knowledge, planners, or architecture promotion; altering unrelated experiments.
- **Required tests:** option validation; paired seed/world-stream equivalence; observer-field isolation; bounded support conservation/change; each mode activates only its named process; inactivity safeguard; restoration probe; row schema and counts; deterministic repeated run; full suite.
- **Required execution workflow/artifacts:** 400-row JSONL plus summary; byte-identical replay JSONL; commit SHA and BEAM environment; per-variant and paired criteria; artifact digest and retention; focused and full test results.
