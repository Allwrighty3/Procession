# Archived Solo Dependent Learner Baselines

Status: archived from the active sibling comparison on 2026-07-23.

These controls remain useful historical evidence, but the current experiment is focused on two simultaneously developing learners. They should not be rerun or shown in the default sibling report unless explicitly requested.

## Configuration

- Population: 12 runs per solo condition
- Baby phase: 2,500 ticks
- Participation phase: 2,500 ticks
- Withdrawal phase: 3,000 ticks
- Vitality depletion: 0.014 per world tick
- Participatory teacher: original direct feeding and participation provisioning
- Teacher absent during withdrawal
- Developmental field update gate: deterministic 1% of world ticks
- World-owned finite intent deadline

## Archived results

| Condition | Baby survival | Participation survival | Withdrawal survival | Mean self-intake | Mean caregiver intake | Mean withdrawal intake |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| No teacher, alone | 0/12 | 0/12 | 0/12 | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 |
| Participatory teacher, alone | 12/12 | 1/12 | 1/12 | 6.017 | 35.040 | 2.500 |

## Interpretation retained

The no-teacher solo learner did not survive long enough to develop independent feeding. The participatory teacher enabled baby survival, but only one learner retained enough behavior to survive the later phases.

These results are retained as historical anchors for teacher necessity. They are not part of the active sibling-only comparison because the current question concerns how two concurrent learners affect one another's survival.
