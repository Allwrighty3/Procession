# Experimental Findings

This file preserves useful results from the post-PR #9 learner experiments without maintaining a separate council process.

## Association reversal baseline

The local-adaptive learner substantially reduced mistaken attribution compared with outcome-only adaptation, but it rarely revised an established action pathway after the environment reversed.

- Outcome-adaptive correction: `4/100`
- Local-adaptive correction: `2/100`
- The result separates attribution quality from revision capacity.

## Additional opportunity and attribution

A paired 100-seed factorial tested additional pre-reversal exposure, additional post-reversal opportunity, and the tested attribution variation.

- Control corrected `2/100`.
- Additional opportunity did not materially improve correction.
- The tested attribution change did not improve correction.
- No mechanism was promoted.

## Contradiction magnitude and finite competition

A second paired 100-seed factorial tested the existing contradiction disturbance at normal and doubled magnitude, with and without finite support-conserving competition.

- Control and doubled disturbance each corrected `2/100`.
- Competition variants corrected `1/100`.
- Doubling disturbance increased support removal but did not change behavior.
- Competition slightly reduced obsolete-action frequency but did not improve correction.
- Activity and restoration remained near control, so inactivity and broad erasure did not explain the result.
- Median post-reversal intake was zero in every variant, limiting interpretation.
- A registered failure calculation mixed incompatible units, so the overall result remains inconclusive.

## Retained direction

The existing learner can improve local attribution but still has weak revision capacity. More exposure, stronger contradiction disturbance, and simple finite competition did not solve that problem under the tested reversal world.

Do not expand these experiments into additional governance or evidence infrastructure unless a new test directly supports a concrete simulation or gameplay capability. Future work should favor visible behavior, IEx demonstrations, and bounded tests over additional meta-process.
