# Developmental Stream Observation

This experiment feeds simultaneous bodily, sensory, motor, change, and contact features into a generic micro-node field. It does not require any expected concept to emerge.

## Run

- 720 ticks
- 64 generic micro-nodes
- 25 generated nodes
- 1,358 retained edges
- total edge mass: 1,932.622

## What formed

The field generated nodes with support sets of roughly 17 to 24 micro-nodes. Some nodes were reused hundreds of times and reached the current stability cap. Several later nodes remained weaker and were reused only a few dozen times.

Observer-only feature overlap showed that nodes frequently coincided with combinations involving:

- bodily capacity and temperature bands,
- caregiver proximity bands and their direction of change,
- motor output,
- contact or lack of contact,
- bodily values rising or remaining stable.

These correlations are not node labels or inferred meanings.

## Unexpected result

The field did not merely fail to form expected concepts. It formed substantial structure, but much of it was highly overlapping. Many internal edges also reached the current weight cap.

This suggests the present mechanism is better at consolidating recurring whole-state configurations than at discovering compact, differentiated abstractions.

That is still useful evidence. The next question is not whether a discomfort or contentment node appeared. It is whether generated nodes become increasingly distinct, reusable, and relationally economical as experience continues.

## Current limitations

- Exact recurring activation signatures trigger consolidation.
- Generated nodes do not yet inhibit redundant neighboring consolidations.
- Edge saturation makes relative structure harder to interpret.
- No merge, dissolution, or differentiation operation exists yet.
- Feature overlap can only suggest which raw inputs supported a node.

The result should therefore be treated as proof that cohesive structure can form from the stream, not proof that the resulting structure is cognitively useful.
