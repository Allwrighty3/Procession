# Embodied Caregiver Regulation Findings

## Question

Can caregiver proximity-seeking emerge without an authored follow force or a rule saying that bodily quantities must be maintained?

## Design

The child has no parent-direction pressure and no hunger-to-resource direction mapping.

Its body contains ordinary causal quantities:

- available capacity,
- temperature,
- fatigue,
- structural strain,
- unresolved activation.

Activity consumes capacity and produces fatigue. Cold and low capacity weaken motor output and increase unresolved activation. Caregiver contact transfers warmth, provides limited capacity, and improves recovery.

Weak seeded motor fluctuations provide the only initial movement. A local cue-motor trace can be retained only when movement increases caregiver cue strength and regulation subsequently reduces unresolved activation.

Three matched 20-seed, 1,800-tick conditions were run:

1. moving, regulating caregiver;
2. moving but non-regulating caregiver;
3. no caregiver.

## Results

```text
regulated:   survived=0/20 lifetime=983 intake=0 resource_visits=0 cue_reuse=0 memory=0 pre_departure_distance=2
unregulated: survived=0/20 lifetime=105 intake=0 resource_visits=0 cue_reuse=0 memory=0 pre_departure_distance=2
no_parent:   survived=0/20 lifetime=105 intake=0 resource_visits=0 cue_reuse=0 memory=0 pre_departure_distance=0
```

## Interpretation

Caregiver regulation was physically meaningful. It increased median lifetime from 105 to 983 ticks without an explicit survival objective.

It did not produce attachment or following. The child retained no caregiver cue-motor traces and performed no independent resource-seeking after caregiver departure.

The failure identifies a missing temporal bridge. Regulation happens after contact, while the movement and weaker caregiver cues that preceded contact have already disappeared from the immediate update. Requiring one transition to both increase the cue and immediately produce measurable relief is too narrow.

The next mechanism should not add a follow-parent drive. It should preserve short decaying eligibility traces for recent sensory and motor activity. Later bodily regulation can then reinforce whatever recent local pathways were still active, without receiving causal truth or a caregiver label.

A suitable next comparison is:

- immediate-only learning, as tested here;
- short sensory-motor eligibility traces;
- traces with delayed caregiver regulation;
- matched visible-but-unregulated and no-parent controls.

The negative result should be preserved. It shows that real bodily care alone is insufficient when the substrate cannot connect delayed consequences to preceding perception and action.
